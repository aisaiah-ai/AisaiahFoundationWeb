# AIsaiah Foundation
# Website Redesign

### Board & Internal Presentation
### April 2026

---

## Why We Did This

The previous website was **clean and informational** but it wasn't doing its job.

Visitors could learn *about* the foundation, but the site didn't answer:

- **What should I do right now?** &rarr; No clear primary action
- **What is the actual product?** &rarr; App wasn't prominent
- **Why should I care personally?** &rarr; Messaging felt institutional
- **Can I trust this?** &rarr; No social proof

We needed to shift from a **nonprofit brochure** to a **product-led movement**.

---

## The Core Insight

People don't visit our site to read about a nonprofit.

They come because they want to **grow in their faith**.

Everything we changed flows from one question:

> **"How does this help me build a daily relationship with God?"**

---

## Our New Framework

We unified the entire site around **three pillars**:

<div class="two-col">
<div>

### The Pillars

**Pray** &mdash; connect with God daily

**Reflect** &mdash; engage with Scripture and journal

**Serve** &mdash; live faith through action

</div>
<div>

### The Mission (Public-Facing)

*Helping people build a daily relationship with God through prayer, reflection, and service.*

This line now appears in the **hero**, **footer**, **mission section**, and across all pages.

</div>
</div>

---

## What Changed: The Homepage

**Before:** 7 generic sections (Hero, Mission, Features, Partnerships, Blog, FAQ, CTA)

**After:** 10 conversion-optimized sections following a proven flow:

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Hero | Hook with problem + solution + CTA |
| 2 | Trust Bar | Credibility signals |
| 3 | Value Proposition | Pray, Reflect, Serve pillars |
| 4 | Product Experience | Daily rhythm + app showcase |
| 5 | How It Works | 3 steps to get started |
| 6 | Testimonial | Social proof |
| 7 | Community | Churches & organizations |
| 8 | Mission | Why we exist |
| 9 | Donation | Support the cause |
| 10 | Final CTA | Close with download |

---

## The New Hero

### Before
- Generic tagline: *"Turn screen time into sacred time"*
- App screenshots with marketing text baked in
- No clear primary call to action

### After
- **Problem-based headline:** *"Struggling to stay consistent in your faith?"*
- **Clear solution:** prayer, reflection, and service
- **Dominant CTA:** "Download the App"
- **3 elegant app preview cards** representing each pillar

Visitors now understand what AIsaiah is in **3 seconds**.

## Why Consistent Language Matters

When every page uses the same framework, visitors:

1. **Understand faster** &mdash; one message, not ten
2. **Remember us** &mdash; "prayer, reflection, and service" sticks
3. **Trust us more** &mdash; consistency signals intentionality
4. **Tell others** &mdash; they know exactly what to say about us

The mission line appears **15+ times** across the site, reinforcing the same message from homepage to footer.

## Technical Improvements

### Architecture
- **Content separated from UI** &mdash; all homepage copy lives in one typed file (`homepage.ts`)
- **Reusable components** &mdash; `AppPreviewCard`, `SectionHeading`, modular sections
- **CMS-ready** &mdash; swap content imports with API data when ready

### SEO & AI-Search
- Semantic section IDs for anchor navigation
- `aria-label` on every section for accessibility
- `MobileApplication` JSON-LD structured data
- Metadata aligned across all pages
- Updated OpenGraph image with new messaging

### Accessibility
- Proper heading hierarchy (one H1, clear H2s)
- Decorative elements marked `aria-hidden`
- Keyboard-navigable, screen-reader friendly
